## How commercial licensing works

Commercial licenses are **not sold off a price list**. Each request
is handled individually so that the terms fit the actual use case.

The general process:

1. You send a request (see _Contact_).
2. The author reviews the proposed use.
3. If the author chooses to grant a license, the scope, duration,
   and fee are **negotiated directly** between you and the author.
4. A written agreement is signed before any commercial use begins.

Possible license shapes (examples, not an obligation):

| Model | Typical use case |
|-------|------------------|
| Per-device royalty | Hardware vendors shipping gateways or repeaters |
| Annual site license | Service providers, SaaS, hosted offerings |
| Perpetual source license | One-time embedding into a closed product |
| OEM / redistribution | Reselling as part of a larger product line |

The author reserves the right to decline any commercial licensing
request, for any reason, without being obliged to state that reason.

typically receive an initial reply within a few weeks.
